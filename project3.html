Overview

Designed a custom, application-specific PCB in KiCad to support an AI camera module; the dimension-constrained layout simplified the mounting process and reduced installation time.

Highlights

  • Designed a compact LED ring-light PCB in KiCad, taking the schematic through to a manufacturable circular layout optimized for mechanical fit and clean mounting.
  • Implemented a configurable LED population option: the same board supports either 8 LEDs or 4 LEDs, enabling flexible BOM/cost and brightness configurations.
  • Supported dual supply operation based on LED count: 24 V operation when populated with 8 LEDs, and 12 V operation when populated with 4 LEDs (as per camera requirement).
  • Integrated a simple configuration method using a solder jumper to adapt the circuit to the chosen build option.

Role & responsibilities

  • Captured and developed the full schematic (LED string, supply connector, transistor stage, resistors, and solder-jumper configuration).
  • Performed PCB layout for a ring form-factor, routing traces around the perimeter while maintaining placement symmetry for uniform illumination.
  • Selected footprints and ensured DFM readiness (component placement, clearances, connector access, and assembly practicality for the ring geometry).
  • Populated the PCB and performed SMD soldering (hand assembly/rework as needed), then powered up and tested the ring light to validate correct LED operation and configuration for both 12 V (4‑LED) and 24 V (8‑LED) variants.

End result

  • Delivered a single, reusable ring-light PCB design that can be built as a 4‑LED (12 V) or 8‑LED (24 V) variant using the same base hardware.
  • Produced a mechanically friendly, dimension-constrained board that simplifies mounting and reduces assembly friction during integration.
  • Improved AI camera image quality by providing stable, uniform illumination on the target area, reducing shadows and exposure variation—helping the vision pipeline achieve more consistent detection performance (especially in low-light/variable ambient lighting conditions).
